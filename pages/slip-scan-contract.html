1. INFRASTRUCTURE & BACKUP PROTOCOLS

The Service Provider utilizes a managed Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS) infrastructure powered by Supabase, hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS).

1.1. Data Sovereignty & Storage: Data is stored in AWS enterprise-grade data centers compliant with ISO27001 and SOC2 standards.

1.2. Backup Frequency: Automated daily full backups of all production databases are encrypted at rest and stored in geographically redundant AWS Availability Zones.

1.3. Business Continuity (RPO): The Service Provider maintains a backup schedule ensuring that, in a standard recovery scenario, data loss will not exceed 24 hours (Recovery Point Objective).

2. DISASTER RECOVERY & SERVICE AVAILABILITY

2.1. Server-Side Recovery (RTO): In the event of a service disruption within the Service Provider's direct control, the Service Provider aims to restore backend functionality within 4 to 24 hours.

2.2. Platform Dependency (App Stores): The Client acknowledges that updates to mobile application binaries (APK/IPA) are subject to the review processes of Google Play and Apple App Store. The Service Provider commits to submitting critical security patches within 24 hours of resolution, but cannot guarantee third-party review times.

2.3. Third-Party Dependency: The Service relies on upstream providers (Supabase/AWS). The Service Provider is not liable for downtime caused by catastrophic failure or extended outages of these upstream providers.

2.4. Grid Failure & Force Majeure: The Service Provider shall not be liable for service interruptions caused by Force Majeure events, including but not limited to national grid collapse, extended Load Shedding beyond reasonable backup battery capacity, fire, flood, or acts of government.

3. POPIA COMPLIANCE & DATA PRIVACY

3.1. Roles: For the purposes of the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("POPIA"), the Client is the "Responsible Party" and the Service Provider is the "Operator".

3.2. Processing Limitation: The Service Provider agrees to process Personal Information only with the knowledge and authorization of the Client and treats all Personal Information as confidential.

3.3. Security Measures: The Service Provider warrants that it has implemented appropriate, reasonable technical and organizational measures to prevent loss of, damage to, or unauthorized destruction of Personal Information (Section 19 of POPIA).

3.4. Sub-Operators: The Client consents to the Service Provider utilizing third-party cloud infrastructure (Supabase/AWS) as sub-operators, provided these sub-operators adhere to international security standards (GDPR/ISO27001).

4. DATA RETENTION & TERMINATION

4.1. Active Term: Data is retained indefinitely while the Agreement is active.

4.2. Post-Termination: Upon termination, Client Data is retained in a dormant state for 30 days.

4.3. Permanent Deletion: After 30 days, all Client Data is explicitly purged from live production environments in accordance with Section 19 of POPIA.
